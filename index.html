FAQs

What is share averaging?
Share averaging (or cost averaging) is a strategy where you buy the same stock at different prices over time. The average cost per share helps you understand your true entry price across all purchases.
How is the average cost calculated?
Total Cost ÷ Total Shares. Add up the cost of all purchases (quantity × price) and divide by the total number of shares owned.
Does this include brokerage fees or taxes?
No. This calculator only uses the share price you enter. For a true cost basis, add any brokerage commissions or taxes to the price per share before entering it.
Can I use this for commodities, crypto, or ETFs?
Yes. It works for any asset you can buy in units — stocks, ETFs, crypto, mutual funds, or commodities. Just enter the quantity and price per unit.
Is my data saved anywhere?
No. All calculations happen in your browser. Nothing is stored on a server. Your entries will be lost if you refresh the page.
What if I sell some shares?
Remove those entries from the list to reflect your remaining holdings. The average will recalculate based on what's left.
